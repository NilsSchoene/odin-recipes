Tofu Doner Kebab

"Döner Kebab" by Tim Reckmann is licensed under CC BY 2.0 .

Description

Doner is a very popular dish in my home country Germany. You will have a hard time finding a city without a dedicated turkish restaurant selling you a fresh doner in different forms and variations. The most popular variation fills a piece of flatbread with thinly sliced meat, freshly cut vegetables and tzatziki sauce.

Despite its popularity it is quite hard to find vegan variants of this dish. Especially in smaller cities or towns. This recipe allows you to cook yourself a doner in almost no time for a low price by substituting the meat with tofu.

Ingredients

  • 1 piece of flatbread or pita bread
  • store bought tzatziki or any other vegan sauce you like (or this self made Vegan Tzatziki)
  • 200 g firm tofu
  • 8 ml vegetable oil
  • 30 ml soy sauce
  • 7 g light brown sugar
  • 3/4 tsp (toasted) sesame seeds
  • tomato
  • cucumber
  • green salad of your choice
  • coleslaw (can be store bought)
  • shredded red cabbage

Steps

  1. Slice the tomato, cucumber and green salad into pieces of your desired size.
  2. Press the tofu with a paper towel to remove any excess liquid from it and shred it with a box grater or slice it in thin stripes with a cheese slicer or a vegetable peeler.
  3. Heat up the vegetable oil in a pan. Once heated add the sliced or shredded tofu and cook for 3-4 minutes until it gets a light brown color.
  4. Add the soy sauce, light brown sugar and sesame seeds to the pan and cook for another 1-2 minutes.
  5. Heat up the bread in the oven or toast it for a short time.
  6. Cut open your bread but make sure to not fully cut off the upper part from the lower part and spread some tzatziki inside.
  7. Lastly fill up the bread with all remaining ingredients and optionally top again with some sauce. Enjoy!
